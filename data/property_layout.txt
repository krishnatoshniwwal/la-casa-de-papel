#SECURITY LOG: THE VELVET ACE
#ID : BP-11
#SENSITIVITY : PUBLIC


## OVERVIEW ## 
Property Name : The velvet ace resort and casino
The resort covers a total of 2,500,000 square feet. 
The hotel towers has 1200 rooms spread across 22 floors.
The casino floor covers an area of 150,000 square feet.
The resort has a basement parking facility of 2500 vehicles split in 2 floors B1,B2 each with elevator access to the casino floor and the hotel lobby.
There is also a B3 for casino operations.
The casino is on the ground floor. 
The first floor covers the hotel lobby and the hotel lounge.
The second and third floor covers 3 fine dine restaurants, 1 convention hall and 1 entertainment theatre. 
The floors 4-25 covers the hotel rooms. 
The B2 and B1 level also covers electrical transformers, water systems, waste management and loading docks.
Guests can access B2 and B1 via elevators which go from B2 to level 1 (hotel lobby).
Separate staff-only elevators which run from B2 to level 25 (top floor).
B3 is an ultra secure sub basement which covers count room, survellience HQ, security command center and storage vaults. 
B3 can be accessed only via secure service elevators requiring keycard+biometric and B3 is accessible only after multi-layer authentication.

## CASINO LAYOUT ## 
The casino floor covers an area of 150,000 square feet.
The casino is located on the ground floor (Level 0).
The casino floor is divided into slot machines, table games, high-limit gaming, bars, cashier area, restrooms, ATMs, and retail sections.
The slot machine zone occupies approximately 90,000 square feet and contains around 1,800 machines arranged in dense clusters with curved pathways.
Low-denomination machines are placed near entrances, while higher-denomination machines are located deeper inside.
The table games area covers approximately 35,000 square feet and consists of around 120 tables.
Tables are grouped into pits of 10–15 tables, each monitored by a pit boss.
The high-limit gaming area covers approximately 10,000 square feet and is located in a discreet section with controlled access.
The casino floor includes 5 bar and lounge areas distributed across the gaming zones.
The cashier (cage) area is centrally located with 20–30 cashier windows and secure access to B3.
Restrooms are placed along the edges of the casino floor behind gaming zones.
The casino includes 6 ATM clusters positioned near high-traffic areas.
Retail sections are located along the perimeter and near exit pathways.
Multiple entrances connect the casino floor to street access, parking levels (B1/B2), and the hotel lobby (Level 1).
The layout uses curved pathways and indirect routes to avoid straight lines and increase player movement.
Exit paths are not directly visible and require movement through gaming or retail areas.

## HOTEL LAYOUT ## 
Level 1 contains the hotel lobby, reception, concierge services, and hotel lounge.
It includes check-in counters, waiting areas, bell desk, and luggage storage.
Guest elevators and escalators to the casino are located here.
Level 2 contains fine dining restaurants and premium hospitality spaces.
It includes 3 restaurants, private dining rooms, kitchens, and service corridors.
Level 3 contains a convention hall, an entertainment theatre, and additional dining spaces.
It includes pre-function areas, seating zones, stage/backstage areas, and event support rooms.
Levels 4–10 contain standard hotel rooms.
Each floor includes 40–60 rooms, central corridors, housekeeping rooms, and service access points.
Levels 11–18 contain deluxe rooms and upgraded accommodations.
Rooms are larger with improved layouts, better views, and additional amenities.
Levels 19–23 contain premium rooms and junior suites.
These floors have lower room density and enhanced privacy features.
Levels 24–25 contain luxury suites and VIP accommodations.
These floors include large suites, private access control, and dedicated service areas.
Guest elevators connect Level 1 to Level 25 with zoned access for efficiency.
Service elevators connect all floors for housekeeping, room service, and operations.
Each hotel floor includes fire exits, utility shafts, and maintenance access areas.
The podium levels (Level 1–3) act as a transition layer separating public, entertainment, and residential spaces.
The vertical layout ensures efficient movement, privacy in upper floors, and clear separation of functions.
